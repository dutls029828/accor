  1. 1. Let's go!

    The Mercure Les Arcs 1800 Hotel is located in the heart of the Les Arcs ski resort in the French Alps. When you stay here you will find this hotel is ideally located at the base of the slopes. If you walk from the lobby to the lift in the middle of winter, you'll most likely leave footprints in the snow. If someone follows you down the same path, they won't know whose shoe made the footprints. An experienced tracker, on the other hand, will already know a lot about you based on your footprints: the type of shoes you wore, the direction you went, the length of time you were walking, and so on.

    Browsing the internet is somewhat like walking on snow. It leaves traces when browsing multiple websites. Like footprints in the snow, the footprints you leave on the Internet cannot be used to directly identify you. But they do give some clues about you: your computer's configuration, your region, your most spoken languages, your interests, and more. Accor includes the websites you visit, browsing or reading, as well as keeping track of any traces you may have already left when booking a stay at the Mercure Les Arcs 1800 Hotel or one of Accor's other 5,100 hotels worldwide.   2. 2. What is Tracer?

    Cookies are the most common form of tracking programs. A tracer is data that a server uses to send information to a web browser or mobile application, which then returns that information to the originating server. Information can be, for example, session ID, language, expiration date, match field, etc. When active, tracers are used to store information when a user accesses various pages of a website or mobile application, or when this user returns to this website or mobile application at a later time.

    There are different types of tracers on the Internet:
    • Session tracers, which are deleted as soon as you leave your browser or website
    • Persistent tracers, which remain on your device until they expire or until you delete them by changing the settings in your browser.

    On our website, we use cookies set by us and called "first-party cookies" and "third-party cookies", which are cookies from a different domain than the domain of the website you are visiting.

    On the ALL, hotelF1, ibis, Pullman, Novotel, Mercure, MGallery, Business Travel, Meetings, Travelpros, Restaurants, Apartments & Villas, and Sofitel websites, we primarily use cookies and other tracers for the following purposes:

    Essential Cookies

    These cookies are essential for the functioning of the website and cannot be turned off within the company system. These cookies are primarily set only based on your privacy preferences, your actions that correspond to service requests, such as logging in or filling out forms. You may block these cookies in your browser or set notification settings for cookies, but in this case some features of the site may not work.

    Functional Cookies

    This cookie allows personalization and advanced features such as video and live chat. Cookies may be set by companies or third-party service providers added to company pages. Some features may not work properly if cookies are not accepted.

    Performance Related Cookies

    These cookies enable us to measure and improve the performance of our site by identifying the number of visitors and data traffic information. It also checks which pages are most popular or least popular, and lets you see how your visitors are moving through your site. All information collected by cookies is cumulative and anonymous. If you do not allow cookies, we cannot know when you visit our site.

    These cookies are set on the Company's site by the Company's advertising partners. These partners use these cookies to create a profile about your interests and display relevant advertisements on other sites as well. This cookie works by identifying your browser and device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will not be able to experience personalized advertising provided by us on other websites.

These cookies can track your browser across multiple sites and profile your areas of interest. This may affect the content and messages you see on other websites you visit.
